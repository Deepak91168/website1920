Code Of Conduct

For ISTE Members

The following Code of Professional Conduct and Ethics shall be applicable to office bearers and members of all categories of the Society:

  • 1. Members should at all times preserve the dignity and interest of the profession and of the Society by maintaining a high standard of professional and personal conduct

  • 2.They should work in the best interest of the Society;

  • 3.They should neither engage in personal advertisement, nor canvas for an appointment;

  • 4.They should at all times endeavour to establish and maintain the best possible conditions of service for their colleagues;

  • 5.When a Member represents the Society, he should as far as possible state the policy of the Society. If he has any disagreement with a policy of the Society, he may so inform the Society. Dealing With Society

  • 1. Adhere to any reasonable pattern of behaviour accepted by the Society for professional persons.

  • 2.Respect the community in which employed and be loyal to the institute system and the country.

  • 3.Render possible assistance to the development of the Society and use your knowledge and skill for enhancement of human welfare.

For ISTE Members

  • 1. Seek to make professional growth continuous by such procedures as study, research, and attending conferences, professional meetings and continuing education programmes.

  • 2.Make the teaching profession so attractive in ideals and practices that sincere and able young men / women want to enter it.

  • 3.Be punctual and regular in teaching work, correspondence with others and keeping appointments with other persons.

  • 4.They should at all times endeavour to establish and maintain the best possible conditions of service for their colleagues;

  • 5.Encourage one's fellow teachers to adhere to proper ethical behaviour.

  • 6.Speak constructively of other teachers but report honestly to responsible persons in matters involving the welfare of students, institution system and the profession

  • 7.Maintain active membership in professional organisation and through participation, strive to attain the objectives that justify such organisations.

  • 8.Never engage in personal advertisement of canvass for an appointment.

  • 9.Never seek self-enhancement through expressing evaluations or comparisons damaging to other professional workers.

Be careful in the words that you choose. We are a technical community of students & faculties and we conduct ourselves professionally. Be kind to others. Do not insult or put down other members. Harassment and other exclusionary behaviour aren't acceptable. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • 1.Violent threats or language directed against another person.

  • 2.Discriminatory jokes and language.

  • 3.Posting sexually explicit or violent material.

  • 4.Posting other people's personally identifying information.

  • 5.Personal insults, especially those using racist or sexist terms.

  • 6.Unwelcome sexual attention.

  • 7.Repeated harassment of others. In general, if someone asks you to stop, then stop.
